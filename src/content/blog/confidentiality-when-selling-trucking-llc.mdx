For most sellers, the second concern after &ldquo;what&rsquo;s it
worth&rdquo; is &ldquo;how do I keep this quiet.&rdquo; The trucking
industry is small. Drivers talk. Dispatchers talk. Insurance agents
talk. If word gets out that an LLC is on the market, things get
complicated &mdash; drivers leave, brokers shift loads to competitors,
and the LLC&rsquo;s value erodes during the very weeks the deal is
trying to close.

That&rsquo;s why confidentiality is a hard default for us, not an
optional add-on.

## What &ldquo;confidential&rdquo; actually means at Veritor

Concretely:

- **Your name doesn&rsquo;t appear in any post-close marketing.** We
  don&rsquo;t do case studies, testimonials, or social posts naming
  past sellers.
- **Your identity isn&rsquo;t shared with drivers or dispatchers.**
  Phone and email transitions happen quietly, framed as routine
  account updates.
- **Brokers and shippers see continuity, not a sale.** The MC
  authority is the same, the LLC is the same, the coverage is the
  same. From their side, nothing changes.
- **Competitors don&rsquo;t hear it from us.** Period.

## The NDA option

If you want it in writing before sharing anything sensitive, we sign a
mutual NDA before diligence starts. It&rsquo;s a one-page document, no
fees, no negotiation theater. Email us through the contact form and
ask for it &mdash; we&rsquo;ll send a draft within hours.

## When we do have to disclose

There are a few legally-mandated exceptions:

- **Lenders.** If the LLC has active loans, the lender has to be
  informed of the ownership change as part of the payoff. We disclose
  the buyer (us), not your forwarding plans.
- **Insurance carriers.** Same logic &mdash; the policy can&rsquo;t
  re-bind without telling the carrier the LLC&rsquo;s new ownership.
- **Government agencies.** FMCSA needs updated contact info post-close.
  This isn&rsquo;t a public disclosure of a sale &mdash; it&rsquo;s a
  routine update.
- **Our legal counsel.** They review every deal but they&rsquo;re
  bound by attorney-client privilege.

What we don&rsquo;t do: tell brokers, drivers, dispatchers, factoring
contacts, or competitors. Those parties find out as natural relationships
shift after closing, not because we announced anything.

## Why this matters more for Amazon Relay LLCs

If you&rsquo;re selling an LLC with an active Relay contract,
confidentiality has an extra layer: Amazon&rsquo;s onboarding team
treats ownership changes as routine, but only when handled through
proper channels. We coordinate with Amazon directly so the contract
continues without interruption. Drivers running loads under your MC
keep running loads under your MC. The continuity is the entire point.

## What we ask of sellers

In the same spirit, we ask sellers not to discuss the deal with their
drivers, dispatchers, factoring contacts, or competitors during the
diligence and closing window. The reason is purely practical: the
quieter the close, the smoother the transition, the more value the LLC
holds at the moment of transfer.

After closing, you&rsquo;re free to talk about it however you want. But
during the deal &mdash; quiet wins.
