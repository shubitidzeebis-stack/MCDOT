## Preamble — Why This Campaign Works

Campaign 1 targets owners who have already decided to sell. This campaign targets owners one step earlier: they've received an offer (or are afraid of receiving a bad one) and are asking the internet whether they're being treated fairly. That search moment is where trust is won or lost. Veritor's /get-offer wizard — which pulls live FMCSA data and returns a written valuation range — is the single best-matched landing page in this space. No competitor has anything close to it for this intent layer.

The tension: $20/day is roughly 4–8 clicks per day at expected CPCs. Every wasted click is a wasted day. Exact match only, hard negatives, tiny keyword list. No exceptions.

## Section 2 — Recommended Starting Keyword List ($20/day Budget)

**Philosophy:** At $20/day, we get ~4–8 clicks per day assuming $2.50–$5.00 CPCs. Over 30 days that's 120–240 clicks. Statistical significance requires 100+ clicks per keyword to evaluate performance. Therefore: **maximum 15 keywords total, ideally 10–12, across 2 tightly themed ad groups.** Any more and we're diluting data collection.

Kill criteria applied: removed all Cluster 6 (too broad), all phrase-match variants (exact only given budget), all keywords with insufficient search evidence, all keywords that are duplicates or near-duplicates of Campaign 1.

### Ad Group A — "What's It Worth" (Worth + Valuation)

Exact match only. Theme: owner-operators quantifying their asset.

```
[how much is my trucking company worth]
[trucking company valuation]
[trucking business valuation]
[how to value a trucking company]
[free trucking company valuation]
[trucking company valuation calculator]
[trucking company appraisal]
[owner operator business valuation]
```

**8 keywords.** Deliberately excludes `[trucking company valuation multiples]` despite confirmed autocomplete presence. Reason: at $20/day, a keyword that attracts finance researchers, investment bankers, and business students (all of whom look up EBITDA multiples) will burn 30–40% of clicks on irrelevant traffic before you can prune it. The multiples keyword belongs in Phase 2 when the search-terms report gives you enough data to add the right negatives. Replaced here with `[owner operator business valuation]` — same diligence intent but tighter owner-operator framing.

### Ad Group B — "MC Authority Worth + Is This Fair" (Authority + Fairness)

Exact match only. Theme: FMCSA-aware owners and anti-lowball suspicious owners.

```
[what is my mc authority worth]
[mc authority valuation]
[trucking company lowball offer]
[fair price for trucking company]
[second opinion trucking company offer]
[how much is my mc number worth]
[trucking business fair market value]
```

**7 keywords.** Lower volume individually but near-zero competition and extremely high intent. The anti-lowball cluster is the unique wedge this campaign owns. No competitor is bidding on "trucking company lowball offer" or "second opinion trucking company offer" — confirmed via autocomplete (both exist as indexed searches with no suggestion siblings, meaning zero competitive pressure on autocomplete).

**Total starting list: 15 keywords across 2 ad groups. Do not exceed this on a $20/day budget.**

**Tradeoff acknowledged:** Clusters 3 (free appraisal variants), 5 (FMCSA extended), and 6 (retirement planning) are deliberately excluded. They are real keywords but at this budget they would fragment data collection and prevent any individual keyword from reaching statistical significance. Add them in Phase 2 when budget increases to $50+/day.

## Section 6 — Quality Score Notes

Quality Score is determined by expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing page experience. Here is the assessment by keyword cluster against /get-offer:

### Expected Excellent QS (7–10/10)

**[mc authority valuation], [what is my mc authority worth], [how much is my mc number worth]**

Reason: The /get-offer wizard explicitly asks for an MC number and looks it up in FMCSA's database. A searcher typing "what is my mc authority worth" and landing on a page that says "Enter your MC number — we'll pull your FMCSA profile and give you a written valuation" will immediately understand the alignment. Expected CTR will be high because the ad can mirror the query almost word-for-word. Landing page experience will be rated excellent because the page literally does what the keyword implies.

**[free trucking company valuation], [trucking company valuation calculator], [trucking company appraisal]**

Reason: These users are looking for a tool or service that delivers a valuation output. /get-offer IS that tool. The landing page has a wizard with a defined output (written valuation range). Google's crawler will score this landing page highly for these queries because the page is not a lead form pretending to be a calculator — it's an actual data-driven valuation workflow.

**Special flag on [trucking company appraisal]:** "Appraisal" is semantically ambiguous — it defaults to physical-vehicle appraisal in many users' minds (a truck getting appraised for insurance or resale). The negative list covers `-truck appraisal`, `-semi truck appraisal`, `-equipment appraisal` but contamination from "trucking company" + "appraisal" phrasing in vehicle contexts may still bleed through. Pull the Day-7 search-terms report specifically for this keyword and add negatives aggressively. If Day-7 shows >30% non-business-valuation searches, pause it.

### Expected Good QS (5–7/10)

**[trucking company valuation], [trucking business valuation], [how to value a trucking company], [trucking company valuation multiples]**

Reason: These are slightly more informational in intent — some users want content (a blog post explaining multiples), not a tool. The landing page will partially satisfy them (it delivers a valuation, which answers the underlying question) but the mismatch between "educational" query and "wizard" landing page will dampen QS somewhat. Mitigate by writing ad copy that explicitly sets expectations: "Enter Your MC Number. Get a Written Valuation Range." — this pre-qualifies the user before the click.

**[trucking company lowball offer], [fair price for trucking company], [second opinion trucking company offer], [trucking business fair market value]**

Reason: These are emotion/situation driven queries. The landing page isn't a "second opinion service" explicitly — it's a valuation wizard. QS will depend heavily on how well the ad copy bridges the gap (e.g., "Worried You're Being Lowballed? Get a Free Written Valuation"). The wizard itself is the right answer; the ad must make that connection clear. Expect QS 5–7 until the ad copy is tuned via A/B.

### Expected Below-Average QS (3–5/10) — Do Not Include in Starting List

**[how much is a trucking company worth]** (generic, without "my")

Reason: Without "my", this query attracts investors, journalists, analysts, and business students asking a general question — not owner-operators seeking their own valuation. The landing page will be judged as a lead-capture page irrelevant to a generic question. Exclude from starting list; add as exact match only after evaluating search-terms report if "how much is MY trucking company worth" starts driving volume.
