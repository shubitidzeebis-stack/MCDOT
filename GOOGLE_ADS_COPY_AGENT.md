**Strategy: pin P1 and P2; leave P3 open for Google rotation.**

| P1 | H1 — Don't Take a Lowball Offer (27) | Defensive framing is this campaign's wedge. Every reader searching "is my offer fair" or "trucking company valuation" is already suspicious — this line mirrors that emotion immediately. Pin to guarantee the hook always leads. |

swap H2 for H4 "FMCSA-Pulled Number in 90 Sec" in P2. Specificity
sometimes outperforms curiosity with owner-operators who've been burned
by vague promises before.

## 7. Call Extension

**Phone:** 213-789-6878
**Country:** United States

**Recommendation: Business hours only, NOT 24/7.**

Owner-operators searching valuation terms are most active Mon-Sat
6am-8pm local time (pre-load and post-delivery windows). A 24/7 call
extension that routes to voicemail outside staff hours degrades Google's
call-conversion quality signal and trains the algorithm to discount the
extension's performance over time.

**Recommended schedule:**
- Monday-Friday: 7:00 AM - 8:00 PM ET
- Saturday: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM ET
- Sunday: Off (suppress if no weekend coverage)

If Veritor has a live answering service or after-hours call routing,
extend to 7:00 AM - 10:00 PM daily and adjust. The mobile-heavy audience
(age 35-60 trucker) will call in the evening if given the window.

## 8. Bilingual Mini-Ad-Group — Spanish

**Recommendation: YES to Spanish, but NOT within the $20/day Campaign 2
budget. Run it as a separate micro-campaign at $10-15/day.**

**Rationale:**
At $20/day total, splitting EN + ES starves both groups. Each needs a
minimum of $8-12/day to accumulate statistically meaningful click data
within 30 days. Spanish valuation-intent volume is smaller than English,
but CPCs are dramatically lower ($1-3 vs $4-9), and no competitor is
running Spanish acquisition ads with FMCSA-data positioning. ROI
potential: $30-80 CPL vs $200-400 EN CPL.

**Launch timing:** Start EN-only at $20/day. At day 30, check the
search-terms report. Spanish-language queries in TX/CA/FL will appear
organically. At that point either (a) create a second $10-15/day
campaign for ES or (b) reallocate: $14/day EN + $6/day ES.

| Pinning penalty | Minor | Pinning H1+H2 to P1+P2 limits combination count — accepted trade-off for defensive-framing brand safety |
| Headline similarity penalty | Minor | H11 and H12 both contain the phone number; Google's deduplication logic may flag similarity |

### Why GOOD, not EXCELLENT, on upload

1. **Phone-number headline similarity:** H11 ("Call 213-789-6878 Free
   Quote") and H12 ("Questions? 213-789-6878") both contain the phone
   number. Google may flag this as low uniqueness even though the copy
   context differs. H12's framing "Questions?" sufficiently differentiates
   them — this is an acceptable risk.

2. **P1+P2 pinning:** Any pinning limits Ad Strength mechanically.
   Veritor's defensive angle justifies the trade-off, but the score will
   reflect the constraint.

3. **No brand-name headline:** Google sometimes rewards including the
   brand name in a headline for new accounts with no Quality Score
   history yet. Consider adding a 16th candidate — "Veritor Group Buys
   MCs" (21 chars) — as a swap for H14 after 30 days if Ad Strength
   guidance specifically requests a brand headline.

### What pushes to Excellent

- H6 "Trucking Company Valuation" (26) is the key unlock. This
  exact-match anchor headline is what Google's Ad Strength engine
  rewards most. It is already in the lineup — do not remove it.
- Confirm that Google Ads' Ad Strength panel does not flag H11/H12 as
  "too similar" after upload. If it does, replace H12 with "Free
  Appraisal. No Listing." (27 chars) to eliminate the similarity flag
  while keeping CTA coverage.

**Best headline:** H1 — "Don't Take a Lowball Offer" (27 chars)

Reasoning: Mirrors the reader's fear in their exact language. Defensive
framing outperforms aspirational framing with suspicious owner-operators

scrolling because it names what they are already thinking.

trust-builder for an audience that has been told vague ranges by brokers.
